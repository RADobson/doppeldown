## 2. Support Philosophy & Principles

### The DoppelDown Support Promise

We exist because enterprise vendors told SMBs "you're too small for us." Our support must never make a customer feel that way. Every interaction should reinforce: **you matter, your brand matters, and we're here to protect it.**

### Core Principles

#### 1. Be a Guide, Not a Gatekeeper
Most DoppelDown customers are not cybersecurity experts. They're business owners, marketing managers, and MSP operators who need help understanding what a "homoglyph domain" means and why it's dangerous. Our support isn't just "how to use the tool" â€” it's **"how to protect your brand."**

**In practice:**
- Explain the *why* alongside the *how*
- When a customer asks "what does this threat mean?", don't just explain the UI â€” explain the real-world risk
- Offer actionable recommendations, not just feature documentation
- Use plain language; avoid jargon unless the customer speaks it first

#### 2. Speed Wins Trust
In the enterprise brand protection world, support response times are measured in days. DoppelDown should be measured in hours. A fast, helpful response from a founder or early team member creates the kind of loyalty that enterprise vendors can never match.

**In practice:**
- Acknowledge every ticket within 1 hour (even if the full answer takes longer)
- Set clear expectations: "I'm looking into this and will have an answer by [time]"
- If it's going to take more than 4 hours, proactively update the customer
- Use canned responses for common questions â€” but personalize them

#### 3. Proactive > Reactive
Don't wait for customers to reach out with problems. Be the team that emails them saying "Hey, we noticed a new critical threat against your brand â€” here's what it means and what we recommend." This is the single most powerful retention lever in the entire strategy.

**In practice:**
- Monitor scan results for new critical/high threats â†’ proactive outreach
- Check in after first scan, first week, first month
- Send monthly protection summaries (automated)
- Alert customers when their scan coverage changes (new social platform added, etc.)

#### 4. Every Conversation is a Product Signal
Support tickets are the most honest form of product feedback. Track patterns, surface them to product development, and close the loop with customers when their feedback leads to changes.

**In practice:**
- Tag every ticket with a category and sub-category
- Weekly review of ticket themes
- "You asked for it" announcement when shipping features from feedback
- Keep a public changelog and reference it in support replies

#### 5. Transparency is the Brand
DoppelDown's entire market positioning is built on transparency (pricing, features, results). Support must be equally transparent:

**In practice:**
- If there's a bug, say so. Don't hide behind "we're looking into it"
- If a feature doesn't exist yet, say "it's on our roadmap for Q[X]" â€” or "honestly, we haven't planned that yet, but I'm adding it to our list"
- Share scan limitations openly: "Our social media scanning is best-effort for some platforms because they limit automated access"
- Never promise what you can't deliver
