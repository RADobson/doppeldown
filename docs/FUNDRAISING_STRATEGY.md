## 8. Term Sheet Playbook

### Understanding the Instruments

#### SAFE Notes (Pre-Seed / Very Early Seed)

**What:** Simple Agreement for Future Equity. Not debt, not equity â€” a promise of future shares.

**When to use:** Pre-revenue to very early revenue. Quick, cheap, minimal negotiation.

| Term | Founder-Friendly | Standard | Investor-Friendly |
|------|-----------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Valuation Cap** | $6M+ | $4Mâ€“$6M | <$4M |
| **Discount** | 0% (cap only) | 15â€“20% | 25%+ |
| **Pro-rata rights** | No | Yes (>$50K) | Yes (any amount) |
| **MFN clause** | Acceptable | Standard | â€” |
| **Type** | Post-money SAFE | Post-money SAFE | Pre-money SAFE |

**DoppelDown recommendation:**
- Use **YC post-money SAFE** (most standard, well-understood)
- Target **$5Mâ€“$6M valuation cap** for pre-seed
- **No discount** if cap is reasonable
- Grant **pro-rata rights** to investors writing $100K+ checks
- Keep it simple â€” one page, minimal negotiation

#### Priced Equity Round (Seed / Series A)

**When to use:** When raising $1.5M+ and/or you have meaningful traction.

| Term | What It Means | Founder-Friendly | Watch Out For |
|------|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Pre-money valuation** | Company value before investment | Higher = less dilution | Don't overoptimize â€” a fair valuation with great investors > maximum valuation with bad ones |
| **Liquidation preference** | Who gets paid first in exit | 1x non-participating | >1x participating = BAD |
| **Anti-dilution** | Protection if next round is lower | Broad-based weighted average | Full ratchet = very bad |
| **Board seats** | Control over company decisions | Founder majority (2-1) | Investor majority = loss of control |
| **Protective provisions** | Veto rights on major decisions | Standard set only | Excessive provisions = handcuffs |
| **Vesting** | Founder share vesting | 4yr / 1yr cliff, credit for time worked | Vesting reset = unfair for existing founders |
| **Option pool** | Shares reserved for future hires | 10â€“15% | 20%+ option pool = hidden dilution |
| **Pro-rata rights** | Right to invest in future rounds | Major investors only | Universal pro-rata = crowded cap table |
| **Information rights** | Investor access to financials | Quarterly updates | Monthly board packages = admin burden |
| **Drag-along** | Forcing minority to sell | Standard at high thresholds | Low threshold = forced exit risk |
| **Right of first refusal** | Approval for secondary sales | Standard | Overly broad = limits your options |

### Critical Term Sheet Red Flags ğŸš©

| Red Flag | Why It's Bad | What To Do |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **>1x liquidation preference** | In anything less than a huge exit, investors get paid and you don't | Walk away or negotiate to 1x non-participating |
| **Full ratchet anti-dilution** | If you raise a down round, early investors get massively more shares at your expense | Insist on broad-based weighted average |
| **Participating preferred** | Investors get their money back AND their pro-rata share â€” double dipping | Negotiate to non-participating (standard for seed) |
| **Investor board majority** | You lose control of your own company | Founder majority is non-negotiable at seed |
| **Excessive option pool** (>15%) | Dilutes founders before investors' ownership is calculated | Push back to 10â€“12% or justify specific hiring plan |
| **Vesting reset** | Your existing years of work don't count | Insist on credit for time already invested |
| **Redemption rights** | Investors can force you to buy back shares | Unusual and toxic at seed stage |
| **Cumulative dividends** | Increases liquidation preference over time | Not standard at seed, reject it |
