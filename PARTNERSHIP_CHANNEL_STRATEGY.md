## 1. Executive Summary

### The Channel Thesis

DoppelDown's direct-to-customer motion (content, SEO, PLG) will drive early revenue. But **channel partnerships are the fastest path to $50K+ MRR** because:

1. **One MSP partner = 20-50 potential customers** under a single relationship
2. **Partners sell trust** â€” their clients already trust them, so DoppelDown doesn't need to earn it from scratch
3. **Partners cover ground Richard can't** â€” geographic reach, industry verticals, language/culture barriers
4. **Channel revenue compounds** â€” each new partner's clients generate ongoing MRR with near-zero incremental acquisition cost
5. **Richard's cybersecurity network is the distribution advantage** â€” no other brand-protection startup has a founder embedded in the MSP/MSSP ecosystem

## 2. Strategic Rationale â€” Why Channels Win

### The Partner Math vs. Direct Sales Math

**Direct acquisition (Richard doing outbound):**
```
Richard's available selling time: 30 min/day = ~10 hrs/month
Contacts per month: 40-60 (cold email + LinkedIn)
Response rate: 8-15%
Demo-to-close: 25-35%
Customers per month: 1-3

Capacity ceiling: ~36 customers/year (Richard's time is the bottleneck)
```

**Channel acquisition (partners selling for DoppelDown):**
```
Partners onboarded per quarter: 5-8
Each partner has: 30-100 existing clients
Adoption rate per partner: 15-30% of client base
New customers per partner per year: 5-30

With 20 partners: 100-600 customers/year (no Richard time constraint)
```

**Channel partners break the founder-time bottleneck.** Every hour Richard spends enabling a partner multiplies into dozens of customer conversations Richard never needs to have.

## 4. Tier 1: MSP & MSSP Partners

### Why MSPs Are Priority #1

MSPs are DoppelDown's single most important channel for five reasons:

1. **Volume:** A single MSP manages 30-200+ client businesses. Selling to one MSP = selling to dozens of end clients.
2. **Trust:** MSP clients already trust their provider for security recommendations. DoppelDown doesn't need to earn that trust â€” the MSP transfers it.
3. **Stickiness:** MSP-managed clients churn at 1/3 the rate of direct customers because switching MSPs is painful, and brand protection is bundled into the managed service.
4. **Richard's access:** Richard works in cybersecurity presales. He knows MSPs. He attends MSP events. He speaks their language. This is the shortest path to revenue.
5. **LTV multiplication:** MSP accounts grow over time as the MSP adds more client brands. A single MSP account can grow from $200/mo to $2,000+/mo.

## 13. Channel Conflict Management

### Conflict Scenarios & Resolution Rules

Channel conflict is inevitable as both direct and partner sales grow. Clear rules prevent partner frustration and protect relationships.

| Scenario | Rule | Rationale |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Partner and direct both pursuing same prospect** | Partner wins if deal is registered; otherwise first to engage wins | Protect partner investment |
| **Direct customer wants to move to a partner** | Allowed â€” partner receives that account going forward | Don't penalise flexibility |
| **Partner customer contacts DoppelDown directly** | Route to partner (inform customer, cc partner) | Honour partner relationship |
| **Two partners pursuing the same prospect** | First registered deal wins; 14-day head start | Fair first-mover advantage |
| **Partner raises price above DoppelDown list** | Allowed â€” partner owns their pricing with their clients | Partner autonomy |
| **Partner discounts below DoppelDown list** | Discouraged but not prohibited; addressed in QBR | Protect market pricing |
| **Customer churns from partner, signs up directly** | Allowed after 90-day cooling period; partner notified | Can't hold customers hostage |

### Pricing Protection

- **DoppelDown will never undercut partner pricing to win a deal.** If a partner is quoting $199/mo to their client, DoppelDown won't offer the client $149/mo directly. This is sacred.
- **DoppelDown's direct pricing is the "ceiling."** Partners buy below list and sell at list or higher. The published price protects the partner's margin.
- **Promotional pricing applies equally.** If DoppelDown runs a promotion, partners get an equivalent or better offer.

### Deal Registration Process

```
1. Partner submits deal registration:
   - Company name, domain, contact name/email
   - Expected tier and timeline
   - Brief description of opportunity

2. DoppelDown reviews within 48 hours:
   - Check for existing direct relationship
   - Check for competing deal registrations
   - Approve, reject, or flag for discussion

3. If approved:
   - Additional 10-15% discount on that deal
   - 90-day exclusivity window (renewable once)
   - DoppelDown routes any inbound from that prospect to the partner

4. Deal outcome:
   - Closed won â†’ partner receives credited discount
   - Closed lost â†’ registration expires
   - Expired â†’ deal open to other partners or direct
```

## 17. Legal & Compliance Framework

### Partner Agreement (Key Terms)

The partner agreement should be simple, fair, and not require a lawyer to understand.

| Clause | Summary |
|--------|---------|
| **Term** | 12-month auto-renewing; either party can cancel with 30 days notice |
| **Territory** | Non-exclusive; partner can sell anywhere (no geographic restrictions initially) |
| **Pricing** | DoppelDown sets list prices; partner discount per tier level; partner sets own retail price |
| **Commission** | Referral track: 20% of Year 1 payments; paid monthly, net 30; min payout $50 |
| **IP** | DoppelDown owns all product IP; partner granted limited licence to use logos/trademarks for resale |
| **Co-branding** | Partner may co-brand reports and materials per DoppelDown brand guidelines |
| **Confidentiality** | Both parties protect each other's confidential information; standard NDA terms |
| **Data protection** | Partner responsible for client data handling; DoppelDown processes data per Privacy Policy |
| **Liability** | Standard limitation of liability; DoppelDown provides the platform "as-is" to partners |
| **Termination** | Either party, 30 days notice; client accounts continue (transfer to direct or another partner) |
| **Non-compete** | None (partners can carry competing products) |
| **Minimum commitment** | Reseller: 3 client brands within 90 days; Referral: no minimum |

### Data Processing & Privacy

| Item | Approach |
|------|----------|
| **Data controller** | Client is data controller; DoppelDown is data processor; partner is sub-processor (if managing client account) |
| **Data processing agreement** | Included as addendum to partner agreement |
| **Data access** | Partner sees only their clients' data; DoppelDown sees all data for service delivery and support |
| **Data portability** | Clients can export their data at any time |
| **Data retention** | Per tier limits (7 days - 2 years); partner can request extended retention at additional cost |
| **Breach notification** | DoppelDown notifies partner within 72 hours of a data breach affecting partner's client data |
